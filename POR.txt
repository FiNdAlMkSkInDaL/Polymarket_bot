EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
This report documents the results of a statistical audit of the V1-V4 Volume
Expansion Architecture's 17 strategy parameters. Three analyses were performed:

  1. Bootstrap Factor Audit (B=2000, α=0.10) on the 4 confluence factors
  2. Sobol Sensitivity Index recalibration for all 17 parameters
  3. Dynamic Adverse Selection Alpha implementation (vol-scaled t-test threshold)

Key outcomes:
  - 2 of 4 confluence factors (L2 Reliability, Regime Mean-Revert) are NOT
    statistically justified and have been zeroed.
  - 1 factor (Whale) justified but over-weighted; reduced 5→4.
  - 1 factor (Spread Compression) justified; unchanged at 4.
  - Adverse selection monitor upgraded from fixed α=0.05 to dynamic
    α_base × (σ/σ_ref)^γ, clamped to [0.01, 0.15].
  - All tests pass (1004 passed, 6 pre-existing failures unrelated to changes).


================================================================================
SECTION 1: BOOTSTRAP FACTOR AUDIT
================================================================================

Methodology
-----------
  - Source:   scripts/confluence_sensitivity.py marginal factor analysis
  - Test:     One-sided binomial test, H₀: w_on ≤ w_off
  - Samples:  B = 2000 bootstrap replicates per factor
  - α:        0.10 (reject H₀ when p < 0.10)

Results
-------
  Factor                  | p-value | Justified? | Action
  ------------------------|---------|------------|----------------------------------
  Whale (Polygonscan CTF) |  0.041  |    YES     | Reduced 5.0 → 4.0
  Spread Compression      |  0.023  |    YES     | Kept at 4.0 (no change)
  L2 Reliability          |  0.38   |    NO      | Zeroed (3.0 → 0.0), hard gate
  Regime Mean-Revert      |  0.52   |    NO      | Zeroed (3.0 → 0.0)

Interpretation
--------------
  Whale (p=0.041): The factor shows marginal lift in win rate when present, but
  the effect size is moderate. The original weight of 5.0 was aggressive given
  the 600s staleness window of Polygonscan data. Reduced to 4.0.

  Spread Compression (p=0.023): Strongest individual signal among the four
  factors. Spread compression correlates with genuine mean-reversion setups
  where the market is temporarily mispriced. Weight unchanged.

  L2 Reliability (p=0.38): No statistical evidence that L2 reliability as a
  continuous discount improves win rate. However, unreliable books (seq_gap_rate
  > 2%) cause fill uncertainty, so L2 is reclassified as a binary hard gate:
  when l2_reliable=False, ALL confluence discounts are blocked. When True,
  no additive discount is applied (weight = 0).

  Regime Mean-Revert (p=0.52): No marginal lift detected. The regime signal
  remains valuable as DriftSignal Gate 1 (a prerequisite for drift entries),
  but granting an additive EQS discount for regime alone is not justified.
  This also resolves the Flaw 2 double-count where DriftSignal already gates
  on regime_mean_revert=True, making the confluence discount redundant.

SECTION 3: DYNAMIC ADVERSE SELECTION ALPHA
================================================================================

Motivation
----------
  The original AdverseSelectionMonitor used a fixed α=0.05 for the one-sided
  Welch t-test (H₀: μ(PnL_T+15) ≥ 0). This is suboptimal because:

  - In high-vol regimes, noise increases and the same α generates more false
    suspensions (Type I errors), reducing maker participation when spreads are
    widest and most profitable.
  - In low-vol regimes, the test is too permissive, allowing adverse fills to
    accumulate before suspension triggers.

Formula
-------
  α_dynamic = α_base × (σ_rolling / σ_ref)^γ

  Where:
    α_base  = 0.05    (baseline significance level)
    σ_ref   = 0.01    (reference EWMA volatility, calibrated from median 15-min vol)
    γ       = 0.5     (square-root scaling — moderate sensitivity)
    α_min   = 0.01    (floor: never more strict than 1% significance)
    α_max   = 0.15    (ceiling: never more lenient than 15% significance)

  Clamped: α_effective = clamp(α_dynamic, α_min, α_max)

Behaviour
---------
  σ_rolling    | α_dynamic (raw) | α_effective | Effect
  -------------|-----------------|-------------|------------------------------
  0.005        |  0.035          |  0.035      | Low vol → stricter → faster suspension
  0.010        |  0.050          |  0.050      | Normal → baseline behaviour
  0.020        |  0.071          |  0.071      | High vol → more lenient
  0.050        |  0.112          |  0.112      | Very high vol → near ceiling
  0.100        |  0.158          |  0.150      | Extreme → clamped at ceiling

  Fallback: When vol_provider returns None or 0.0, α_effective = α_base (0.05).

Implementation
--------------
  File: src/trading/adverse_selection_monitor.py
    - VolProvider = Callable[[str], float | None]
    - __init__ accepts vol_provider, vol_ref, alpha_gamma, alpha_min, alpha_max
    - _dynamic_alpha(market_id) computes and clamps α
    - _recompute_stats uses effective_alpha instead of fixed self._alpha
    - Log includes effective_alpha field

  File: src/bot.py
    - _vol_provider_for_monitor() closure → agg.rolling_volatility_ewma
    - All 7 params wired from StrategyParams into monitor constructor
